10 Questions to Ask Your Divorce Lawyer

Known for being home to Middle Tennessee State University and having a rich history, Murfreesboro residents get to enjoy family life and breathtaking nature views as they navigate their daily lives.

However, when dealing with personal legal hardships, it can be difficult to know where to begin.

Broaching the topic of divorce with your spouse can make you feel isolated, but it’s important to remember that you aren’t alone. 12% of men and 13% of women in Murfreesboro were divorced in 2024. While this process can be difficult to manage without a lawyer, it was made to be accessible with the right help. After doing online research or seeking a referral from a trusted loved one, be sure to ask the lawyer:

Have You Handled Cases Similar to Mine in the Past?

Every divorce is different and requires a different legal strategy. For example, if you have a history of domestic violence in your relationship or intense emotions involved, you’ll need a lawyer with previous experience in those situations.

What Is Your Approach to Divorce Cases?

Not every lawyer handles a divorce the same way. You’ll want to understand their unique process and whether they’ll closely involve you or handle most of the case on your behalf. If how they want to approach your case doesn’t align with your needs, it may not be a good fit.

How Do You Handle a Case to Prevent It From Escalating Unnecessarily?

Many divorce issues can be resolved through mediation if you and your spouse can communicate healthily. However, if your lawyer plans to stand their ground without negotiating, it may make it difficult to end up with good results.

How Often Do Your Cases Actually Go to Trial, and What Are the Outcomes of Those Cases?

If your attorney has no trial experience or a history of poor outcomes in cases, it may be a red flag for your divorce.

Do I Get Direct Communication With You, or With a Paralegal or Receptionist?

Divorces often come with intense emotions and concerns. You deserve to work with an attorney who is readily available to answer your questions.

Can You Walk Me Through the Entire Legal Process?

If your attorney only offers vague explanations or won’t explain confusing legal concepts, it may be a red flag.

What Are the Realistic Outcomes I Should Expect?

While every divorce case is different and every couple’s financial and familial situation is unique, your attorney should be able to provide insight into what they believe can be accomplished in your case.

How Does Payment Work?

Before hiring an attorney, it’s vital to clearly understand how they handle pricing. Every law firm is different, and if they don’t explain the price of their legal services, it may not be a good fit.

What Challenges Do You Expect During My Case?

Again, while every case is unique, your attorney should have some understanding of what legal pushback you may face during legal proceedings. It’s important to understand what to expect during your case so you aren’t blindsided at any point. You deserve to navigate your case with confidence.

Why Should I Choose You Over Other Divorce Lawyers?

Your lawyer should be able to explain the benefits of working with their firm over local competitors. There is a level of trust that you and your lawyer share. Like a job, you should be interviewing your lawyer to determine their compatibility with your needs.

FAQs

What Should I Do Before Meeting With a Lawyer to Discuss My Divorce Case?

Before meeting with a lawyer to discuss your divorce case, be sure to read their law firm’s Google and Yelp reviews from past clients, as these unbiased reviews will offer insight into how the lawyer acts. Make sure the law firm has practice area pages about divorce and family law, including child custody and alimony, if applicable.

How Is Property Divided in a Tennessee Divorce?

In a Tennessee divorce, marital property is divided based on what the couple can agree on, or what the court considers equitable division. In 2024, the average home value in Woodbury was $402,100. The marital home tends to be a couple’s highest-value asset, requiring careful handling during legal proceedings.

Will I Have to Pay Alimony in My Divorce Case?

The amount you will need to pay in alimony is dependent on several factors. You could pay as little as nothing, but it is up to the determination of the court, which will factor in your income, your expenses, whether you must care for anyone with special needs, and more. To estimate the amount of alimony you may need to pay, speak with your lawyer about your specific circumstances, who may be able to estimate the amount.

Where Do I File My Divorce Case in Murfreesboro?

In Murfreesboro, you can file your divorce case at the Rutherford County Circuit Court, located at 116 West Lytle Street. Before filing for a divorce, hire a divorce lawyer to help explain the legal process and what state laws apply to your situation. Not every lawyer is going to be right for your unique needs.
