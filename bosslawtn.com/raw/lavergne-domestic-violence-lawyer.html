An Overview of Domestic Violence in Tennessee

Domestic violence impacts women, children, and men across the country every day. According to recent data, more than one in three women and one in six men have been victims of some form of sexual violence, stalking, or physical violence from a partner in their lifetime. Because domestic violence is so prevalent throughout the country, Tennessee works to protect victims and provide them with various tools to protect themselves.

According to Tennessee law, domestic abuse can occur against:

  • Current or former spouses
  • People who are living together, whether they are adults or minors
  • People who are dating or who have prior intimate relationships
  • People related by blood, through adoption, or by marriage or divorce

Filing a Protective Order in Tennessee

If you or a loved one is experiencing violence in the home, you have options. One of the strongest tools domestic violence victims can implement is a protective order. A protective order is a legal document in Tennessee that protects victims of domestic violence, stalking, sexual assault, threats, or sexual harassment. There are two types of protective orders in Tennessee:

  1. Temporary Protective Orders (TPOs). Temporary protection orders in Tennessee can help protect someone from immediate threats or abuse. This kind of order can provide a short-term solution until an extended protection order can be granted, if it’s needed.
  2. Extended Protection Order (EPOs). An extended protection order is usually issued after a court hearing and requires evidence. While the temporary protection order is granted without notifying the abuser, the extended protection order allows the accused an opportunity to defend themselves. EPOs generally last longer than TPOs and can be renewed every year.

An experienced domestic violence attorney from Boss Law can help you better understand your options and which protective order may be right for you.

Why Do Most Domestic Violence Cases Get Dismissed?

Domestic violence cases often get dismissed for a variety of reasons. Sometimes the accuser decides to retract their statements or becomes combative with the prosecution. Another reason could be if there wasn’t enough evidence to support the abuse claims. Along with that, if law enforcement violates the rights of the person accused during arrest or at any other time, then the case might be dismissed.

What Is Strong Evidence in a Tennessee Domestic Violence Case?

In general, the more evidence someone has in a domestic violence case, the stronger their case is. Medical records and police reports are among the most useful types of evidence, so it’s important for victims to seek help so that their abuse is documented. Witness testimony, video or photo evidence, and communication with the abuser can also be useful. This can look like emails, texts, and even journal entries.
