## **Which Bankruptcy Is Right For You?**

For personal finances, there are two forms of bankruptcy to consider:

- Chapter 7 bankruptcy: This type of bankruptcy will eliminate virtually all of your unsecured debt. This means credit cards, medical bills, and other forms of debt that aren't secured against a tangible asset. This bankruptcy will not affect student loans from the government except in very rare cases.
- Chapter 13 bankruptcy: This type of bankruptcy doesn't wipe all of your debts away, but instead gives you the opportunity to negotiate with your creditors to create more friendly repayment terms that work with your current income levels.

Which form of bankruptcy is better? From a debt elimination standpoint, the Chapter 7 bankruptcy is better because it is often cheaper for a Murfreesboro attorney to accomplish. The Chapter 13 bankruptcy is more expensive, but it is less damaging to your credit worthiness from a long term perspective in many instances.

## **Do You Qualify For a Chapter 7 Bankruptcy?**

A Murfreesboro attorney can help you determine if you pass the Means Test to qualify for a Chapter 7 bankruptcy. This test was instituted to prevent people from simply filing for bankruptcy instead of paying their bills when they had the ability to do so. Your income, assets, and finances will be looked at as a whole and then compared to other Murfreesboro families. If you qualify, then a Chapter 7 bankruptcy may be recommended. If you do not, then a Chapter 13 bankruptcy can still be pursued as a debt relief option.
