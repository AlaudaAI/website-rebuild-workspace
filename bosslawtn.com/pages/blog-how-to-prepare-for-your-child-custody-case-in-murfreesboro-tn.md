## How Does the Court Determine Child Custody in Tennessee?

In the United States, around [22.2 million children](https://www2.census.gov/library/publications/2025/demo/p60-285.pdf) live in single-parent homes. However, local courts often aim to prioritize joint custody agreements whenever possible. To better prepare for custody proceedings, it's important to understand how judges make custody decisions and what they will consider.

To decide how physical and legal custody will be split between parents in Tennessee, a judge is legally required to consider a variety of factors in order to prioritize the best interests of a child when making their decision. **[Factors that family judges consider](https://www.lrcvaw.org/laws/tnrelocation.pdf) when determining custody in Tennessee include:**

- The strength of the emotional connection that the parent has already made with the child
- Each parent's ability to provide a stable home
- How seriously the parent has taken their parenting responsibilities prior to the court case
- Each parent's emotional, mental, and physical fitness
- Any emotional or physical abuse
- Each parent's work schedule and location
- How the child relates to other siblings or people who dwell in the same house
- Continuity for the child, whether that is remaining in the same school or maintaining a similar community
- The child's preference, if it's reasonable and the child is over the age of 12 years old

## Preparing for Your Child Custody Case in Murfreesboro

Preparing for your child custody case can mean preparing evidence that demonstrates you have the capabilities to care for your child in the way they need. For example, to show that there are strong emotional ties, a parent could demonstrate their daily involvement in their child's life.

Tangible evidence can be very useful in custody cases. **Evidence could look like:**

- School attendance records
- Medical records
- Communication with the other parent
- Testimony and witness statements
- Teacher statements
- Therapy and counseling reports
- A parenting journal

A child custody attorney can review your specific circumstances and help you gather evidence that advocates for your custody rights.

## How to Behave in a Child Custody Case

Many parents don't realize that the way they behave during custody proceedings can impact a final decision. Respectful behavior in a child custody case can make an immense difference in your case, as parents who have outbursts, use foul language, or are rude to judges often negatively influence the decision-making process.

You should, however, **do the following:**

- Attend hearings and meetings on time.
- Wear appropriate attire
- Answer questions respectfully and mindfully.
- Always prioritize your child's needs.
- Follow court orders
- Avoid badmouthing the other parent or trying to turn the child against the other parent.
- Avoid having outbursts, yelling, or using poor language.

## FAQs

### What Is the Biggest Mistake Parents Make in a Custody Battle?

One of the biggest mistakes parents can make during a custody battle is speaking badly about the other parent. This can impact not only the court's view, but most importantly, the child's experience during an already tense custody battle. It is technically illegal to turn a child against their other parent, so talking poorly about your co-parent in court is not wise. You should also avoid lying, yelling, or using foul language in court.

### What Factors Does a Family Judge Consider in a Tennessee Child Custody Case?

A child's best interest is always the judge's primary focus in a custody case. Tennessee defines a variety of factors to consider, which include emotional ties, who is capable of providing a stable home, and how the parent showed up for the child before the custody battle. They also review any evidence of emotional or physical abuse and the character and behavior of each parent.

### What Should You Avoid Saying to a Family Judge During a Custody Dispute?

When speaking to a family court judge, it's important to be aware of what you say. In general, it's advised to avoid volunteering information that hasn't been requested, interrupting the judge, using vulgar language or speech, and misrepresenting the truth. Take the time you need to choose your words carefully so that you don't exaggerate the truth or misspeak. While you might need to be critical of the other parent, do it with thought and care.

### What Can Help Strengthen My Child Custody Case in Tennessee?

Because the court's priority is the child's best interest, showing that that is also your priority can go a long way. You should demonstrate that you have a willingness to co-parent and that you care about the safety and well-being of the child above all else. You can prepare evidence that supports your ability to care for the child, like school and medical records and witness statements.
