## **Finances, Health Care, and Other Directives Are All Part of Wills**

Wills are often one of those legal tasks that many people keep putting off. It's not that people think that they're indestructible. It's simply a difficult subject to discuss because it forces a person's mortality in front of them and forces someone to discuss outcomes that they wish to have happen after they pass away.

A Murfreesboro attorney who specializes in wills can help to navigate these sensitive waters so that they right outcomes can always be achieved. This way, should something unforeseen happen, you'll be able to:

- receive the right level of health care that you wish to have,
- allow for instant guardianship of your children if you're the sole guardian, and
- require the transfer of your assets to your intended heirs.

Without wills in place, it is very possible that your loved ones may need to go to probate in order to enforce your needs. In terms of health care, the lack of a will may mean a medical procedure is performed that you may not want. It may also mean a doctor makes decisions for you instead of a trusted agent that you name. A Murfreesboro attorney can assist you with all of these tasks any many more so that your will is reflective of every desire you have.
