Relationships are difficult, butg sometimes separation is the best choice. However, although the adults have the ability to protect and control their own rights, the children involved in the separation are completely at the mercy of the ebb and flow of the domestic fallout. Often, the result of domestic separation leaves one of the parents with the sole responsibility of raising the child. Or, it's possible that the two were never in a relationship, and the mother finds herself needing to [support a child](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Child_support) that is the product of both adults. Raising a child is not only a job that has no clock-out hours, but it is expensive and requires a lot of hard work. It's hard enough that you have to try and endure the hardships of raising a child alone, you shouldn't have to try and pay for raising the child by yourself.

## Factors Affecting Child Support

In a child support case, the court often considers many factors to determine just how much a parent should be required to pay. These factors include:

- The proposed living situation (sole custody or joint custody)
- The standard of living for the child before separation occurred.
- The child's needs (healthcare, education, daycare, and other needs)
- The non-custodial parent's income and ability to pay.
- The custodial parent's income and needs.

A skilled child support attorney has the expertise to navigate these factors and build a solid case in favor of you and your child.
